title: "Excise Duty Bill 2026 Passed: Shs200/litre Fuel Levy, Sugar/Cement Hikes"
date: 2026-04-22
category: "Policy"
excerpt: "New duties target Shs450bn for budget financing."
source: "The Observer & Parliament"
---

The Parliament of Uganda has passed the Excise Duty Bill 2026, introducing significant tax increases on fuel, sugar, and cement. The Minister of State for Finance (General Duties), Hon. Henry Musasizi, defended the measures as necessary for budget financing.

Key provisions include:
- **Fuel:** Shs200 per litre increase in excise duty on petroleum products
- **Sugar:** New excise duties on sugary beverages and sugar products
- **Cement:** Increased duties on cement products

The government projects that these measures will generate approximately Shs450 billion in additional revenue for the financial year 2026/27.

Musasizi emphasized that thebill is crucial for funding infrastructure development and social programs, adding that the tax increases are modest compared to regional averages and necessary to reduce the budget deficit.

The passage of the Excise Duty Bill represents a significant step in Uganda's fiscal reform agenda aimed at sustainable revenue generation.
