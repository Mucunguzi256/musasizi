---
title: "Parliament Rejects 'Kyeyo' Tax; Musasizi Defends Revenue Proposals"
date: 2026-04-23
category: "Policy"
excerpt: "Government's push to tax remittances from Ugandans abroad faces parliamentary rejection."
source: "Parliament News"
---

Parliament has rejected the government's proposal to tax remittances from Ugandans abroad, with Hon. Henry Musasizi defending the revenue proposals as necessary for national development.

The Minister of State for Finance (General Duties) presented the proposal as part of efforts to broaden the tax base and increase domestic revenue mobilization. The proposed "Kyeyo" tax would have targeted money sent home by Ugandans working abroad.

Musasizi defended the measure, stating that it was designed to capture disguised business transactions and ensure fair taxation of income generated through international trade in services. However, parliamentarians argued that the tax would burden Ugandans in the diaspora and potentially reduce remittance flows, which remain a critical source of foreign exchange.

The debate highlights the ongoing balance between revenue mobilization and economic competitiveness in Uganda's fiscal policy.
